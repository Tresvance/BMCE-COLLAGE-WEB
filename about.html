Our Mission

To provide quality technical education that empowers students with the knowledge, skills, and values needed to become innovative professionals and responsible global citizens. We aim to foster academic excellence, ethical integrity, and a commitment to lifelong learning.

Our Vision

Welcome to BMCE

Baselios Mathews II College of Engineering (BMCE), named after its founder H H Moran Mar Baselios Marthoma Mathews II, is managed by Dr. C T Eapen Trust under the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church. Established in 2002, the college aims to promote academic excellence in Engineering and Technology. Located in the scenic Muthupilakadu in Sasthamcotta, Kollam, Kerala, BMCE features modern infrastructure blending architectural beauty with functionality. Affiliated with APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University, the self-financed institution offers six AICTE-approved undergraduate courses.

Founder

Moran Mar Baselios Marthoma Mathews II (1915 -2006)

Moran Mar Baselios Marthoma Mathews II (1915 - 2006) was the Supreme head of Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church. His Holiness was successor to the Holy Apostolic Throne of St. Thomas and Catholicos of the East and Malankara Metropolitan. His Holiness was born at Perinad in Kollam district of Kerala, and had his theological education at Old Seminary Kottayam and also at Basil Dayara, Pathanamthitta. Later His Holiness joined Bishop's College, Calcutta for his B. D. Degree, and studied Theology at the General Theological Seminary, New York. His Holiness was ordained as deacon in 1938 then as priest in 1941. On 15 May 1953 His Holiness was ordained as bishop Mathews Mar Coorilose at the age of 38 by the Catholicos Baselios Geevarghese II. Mar Coorilose was elevated to the throne of St Thomas as Baselious Marthoma Mathews II, Catholicos of the East and Malankara Metropolitan on 29 April 1991. Due to ill health from old age Mathews II resigned his position as the Catholicos on 29 October 2005. His Holiness died on 26 January 2006, and was interred at Mount Horeb Chapel, in Sasthamkotta.

Management

The success of BMCE is the result of the relentless efforts of many individuals who helped build the institution from the ground up. BMCE is a project of the Dr. C. T. Eapen Trust under the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church. Founded by Dr. C. T. Eapen, a philanthropist and educational visionary, the Trust has made its mark in education, healthcare, and spiritual initiatives. The vision and efforts of the late H.H. Moran Mar Baselios Marthoma Mathews II played a key role in transforming the institution into a center of excellence. His vision fulfilled that of Rev. Dr. C. T. Eapen of Chavadiyil, Adoor, who aspired to form a brotherhood to lead the Trust. The Trust spans 65 acres along the Sasthamcotta freshwater lake, home to the scenic Mar Elia Chapel, where the remains of H.H. Baselios Marthoma Mathews II are entombed. His intercession, along with that of other saints, continues to guide the community. Today, BMCE thrives under the leadership of H.H. Baselios Marthoma Mathews III, the Ninth Catholicos of the East, and is managed by H.G. Yakob Mar Elias Metropolitan.
